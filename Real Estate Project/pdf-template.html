Relevant Definitions:

Purchase Price: The initial cost of acquiring the property before any renovations or additional expenses.

Renovation Cost: The total expenditure incurred for all renovation activities on the property.

Earnest Money: The upfront payment made to secure the property or enter into a contract to purchase the property.

Holding Costs Per Month: The total expenses associated with holding the property for each month, including mortgage payments, property taxes, insurance, and maintenance costs.

Closing Cost Percentage: The percentage used to calculate the total closing costs, which include expenses like title charges, transfer taxes, and other miscellaneous costs related to the property transfer.

Resale Value: The anticipated value of the property after all renovations and improvements have been completed.

Selling Cost Percentage: The percentage of the property's resale value that will be incurred as fees or expenses associated with selling the property, such as agent commissions and closing costs.

Loan Information: Details about the loan taken to finance the property, including the loan amount, interest rate, and the duration of the loan.

Contingency/One-Time Costs: The total amount of expenses that are incurred only once, as well as any additional funds set aside for unexpected or unforeseen costs.

Desired Profit: The amount of profit that the investor aims to achieve from the sale of the property, after accounting for all expenses and costs.
